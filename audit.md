# ARYO Site Audit

Reviewed on April 24, 2026.

Sources used:
- [aryo.london](https://aryo.london)
- [Instagram link surfaced on the live site](https://instagram.com/aryolondon)

## What already works

- The brand position is strong and specific: luxury streetwear, limited production, made in London, rooted in Persian heritage.
- The product imagery is excellent. The coat, jacket, and trouser visuals immediately signal control, quality, and discipline.
- The language has a real point of view. Phrases around restraint, scarcity, and intentional construction already feel more premium than most early-stage labels.
- The Pennicilla material story is genuinely distinctive and worth leading with more boldly.

## What is weakening the live experience

- The homepage feels too abstract for too long. It communicates mood before it communicates product, which slows down understanding for a first-time visitor.
- Core brand proof is split across separate routes. About, collection, support, and waitlist each hold useful information, but the story is fragmented instead of cumulative.
- Public visitors appear to be pushed toward a launch gate too early. The waitlist ask lands before the craftsmanship, material story, or collection structure has fully earned it.
- Navigation is elegant but low-friction discovery is limited. Too much important information is one interaction too far away.
- Some trust-building details are buried in product or support views instead of being visible in the main narrative.
- The canonical tag in the live HTML points to `https://aryo.com` while the public site is `https://aryo.london`, which is not ideal for clarity or SEO.
- The live open-graph preview image still appears to show “Launching 2025,” which is stale on April 24, 2026.

## Direction For The Remake

- Keep the dark editorial tone, but bring the value proposition and the collection into the first screen.
- Combine the best parts of the current About, Collection, Support, and Waitlist pages into one stronger landing flow.
- Make the Pennicilla material story feel central rather than secondary.
- Show both colourways and all four launch pieces clearly.
- Keep the early-access CTA, but place it after visitors understand the product and point of view.
